The biggest galaxies

And fun facts about them

IC 1101

IC 1101 is the largest galaxy in the universe. The galaxy is elliptical lenticular and appears as golden yellow colors. the galaxy more bright is located more than 1.04 billion light-years from Earth.

Phoenix Cluster

Phoenix Cluster is one of the most solid groups of galaxies in the universe. It is located around 5.7 billion light years from Earth. A elliptical galaxy is composed of medium and dark intra-group matter.

NGC 262

NGC 262 is located 202 million light-years from Earth, which is one of the largest known spiral galaxies. The diameter of the galaxy is approximately 1.3 million light years.

NGC 4889

NGC 4889 is also famous as “Coma B”, a super giant galaxy elliptical located approximately 308 million light-years from Earth. It was also discovered by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I in 1785.

NGC 4874

NGC 4874 also known as “Coma A” was discovered in 1785 by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I. It is 350 million light years from Earth. It is a smooth, ball-shaped galaxy surrounded by a circle of stars.

A2261-BCG

A2261-BCG is located approximately 3 billion light years from Earth. It is elliptical in shape and was found by the Hubble Space Telescope. A2261-BCG is one million light-years in diameter.
